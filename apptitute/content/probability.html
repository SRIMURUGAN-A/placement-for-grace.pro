Probability

Concept Overview:

Probability is a branch of mathematics that deals with calculating the likelihood of an event happening. It is used to predict uncertain outcomes. Probability can range between 0 and 1, where 0 means an event is impossible, and 1 means an event is certain.

The probability of an event happening can be calculated using the formula:

  • Probability Formula: Probability of an event (P) = Number of favorable outcomes / Total number of possible outcomes

Types of Problems

Some common types of probability problems include:

  • Finding the probability of a specific outcome.
  • Calculating the probability of multiple independent events occurring together.
  • Determining probabilities involving conditional events.

Detailed Example

Example Scenario:

Imagine you have a fair six-sided die, numbered 1 through 6. What is the probability of rolling a 4?

Solution:

- Number of favorable outcomes (rolling a 4) = 1
- Total number of possible outcomes = 6
- Probability = (Number of favorable outcomes) / (Total number of possible outcomes)

Solution Steps:

Probability = 1 / 6 ≈ 0.167
Answer: The probability of rolling a 4 is approximately 0.167.

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

A jar contains 5 red balls, 3 blue balls, and 2 green balls. What is the probability of randomly selecting a blue ball?

Solution:

Total balls = 5 + 3 + 2 = 10
Probability of blue ball = 3 / 10 = 0.3
Answer: The probability of selecting a blue ball is 0.3.

Problem 2:

In a deck of 52 playing cards, what is the probability of drawing an Ace?

Solution:

There are 4 Aces in a deck of 52 cards.
Probability of drawing an Ace = 4 / 52 = 1 / 13 ≈ 0.077
Answer: The probability of drawing an Ace is approximately 0.077.

Problem 3:

A coin is tossed twice. What is the probability of getting two heads?

Solution:

Possible outcomes = HH, HT, TH, TT (4 total outcomes)
Probability of getting two heads = 1 / 4 = 0.25
Answer: The probability of getting two heads is 0.25.

Problem 4:

A number is chosen randomly from 1 to 10. What is the probability that it is a prime number?

Solution:

Prime numbers between 1 and 10 are 2, 3, 5, and 7 (4 prime numbers).
Probability = 4 / 10 = 0.4
Answer: The probability of selecting a prime number is 0.4.

Problem 5:

A bag contains 7 white balls and 3 black balls. What is the probability of picking a black ball?

Solution:

Total balls = 7 + 3 = 10
Probability of black ball = 3 / 10 = 0.3
Answer: The probability of picking a black ball is 0.3.
