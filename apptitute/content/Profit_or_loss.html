Profit and Loss

Concept Overview:

The concept of Profit and Loss is fundamental in business and daily transactions. When you buy an item for a certain price (Cost Price, or CP) and sell it for a different price (Selling Price, or SP), you can either make a profit or incur a loss.

Here are the basic terms:

  • Cost Price (CP): The price at which an item is purchased.
  • Selling Price (SP): The price at which an item is sold.
  • Profit: If SP > CP, Profit = SP - CP.
  • Loss: If CP > SP, Loss = CP - SP.
  • Profit Percentage: (Profit/CP) * 100
  • Loss Percentage: (Loss/CP) * 100

Types of Problems

Common types of profit and loss problems include:

  • Finding profit or loss given the cost price and selling price.
  • Calculating selling price based on desired profit or loss percentage.
  • Determining cost price given selling price and profit/loss percentage.

Detailed Example

Example Scenario:

Imagine Sarah bought a watch for $80 and later sold it for $100. She wants to know whether she made a profit or incurred a loss and by what percentage.

Solution:

- Cost Price (CP) = $80
- Selling Price (SP) = $100
- Since SP > CP, Sarah made a profit.

Solution Steps:

Profit = SP - CP = $100 - $80 = $20
Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) * 100 = (20 / 80) * 100 = 25%
Answer: Sarah made a 25% profit on the watch.

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

John bought a bicycle for $150 and sold it for $180. Did he make a profit or incur a loss? Calculate the percentage.

Solution:

CP = $150, SP = $180.
Profit = SP - CP = 180 - 150 = $30.
Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) * 100 = (30 / 150) * 100 = 20%
Answer: John made a 20% profit.

Problem 2:

A shopkeeper bought 20 units of a product for $200 and sold them at $240. What is the profit percentage?

Solution:

CP = $200, SP = $240.
Profit = SP - CP = 240 - 200 = $40.
Profit Percentage = (Profit / CP) * 100 = (40 / 200) * 100 = 20%
Answer: The profit percentage is 20%.

Problem 3:

Lisa sold a dress for $50 at a loss of 10%. Find the cost price of the dress.

Solution:

SP = $50, Loss Percentage = 10%
Loss = (Loss Percentage / 100) * CP
SP = CP - Loss
50 = CP - (10/100)*CP
50 = 0.9 * CP
CP = 50 / 0.9 = $55.56
Answer: The cost price of the dress is $55.56.

Problem 4:

A merchant sold an item for $90 and incurred a 10% loss. What was the original cost price?

Solution:

SP = $90, Loss Percentage = 10%
CP = SP / (1 - Loss Percentage/100) = 90 / (1 - 0.1) = 90 / 0.9 = $100
Answer: The cost price was $100.

Problem 5:

Tom bought a painting for $200 and sold it at a 25% profit. What was the selling price?

Solution:

CP = $200, Profit Percentage = 25%
SP = CP * (1 + Profit Percentage/100) = 200 * 1.25 = $250
Answer: The selling price is $250.
