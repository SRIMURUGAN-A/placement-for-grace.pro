Time and Work

Concept Overview:

Time and Work problems involve calculating the amount of work done, the time taken to complete a job, or the efficiency of workers. The basic idea is to understand how long it takes for one or more people (or machines) to complete a job either alone or together.

Formula for Work: If a person can complete a task in 'x' days, then in one day, they complete 1/x of the work.

When multiple people work together, the rate of work is the sum of individual rates. So, the formula for combined work rate is:

  • Combined Work Rate = (Work Rate of A) + (Work Rate of B) + ...

Types of Problems

Common types of time and work problems include:

  • Finding the time taken when people work together.
  • Determining how much work is done by individuals in a certain time.
  • Calculating the required time when more or fewer people join the work.

Detailed Example

Example Scenario:

Alex can complete a task in 10 days, while Ben can finish the same task in 15 days. How long will it take for them to complete the task together?

Solution:

- Alex’s one-day work rate = 1/10
- Ben’s one-day work rate = 1/15
- Combined one-day work rate = (1/10) + (1/15) = (3 + 2) / 30 = 5 / 30 = 1 / 6

Solution Steps:

Time taken to complete the work together = 1 / (Combined work rate) = 1 / (1/6) = 6 days.
Answer: Alex and Ben together can complete the task in 6 days.

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

Emma can complete a project in 12 days, and Liam can complete it in 8 days. How many days will it take them to finish the project if they work together?

Solution:

Emma's one-day work rate = 1/12
Liam's one-day work rate = 1/8
Combined work rate = (1/12) + (1/8) = (2 + 3) / 24 = 5 / 24
Time taken = 1 / (5/24) = 24 / 5 = 4.8 days.
Answer: Emma and Liam can complete the project in approximately 4.8 days.

Problem 2:

A machine can produce 100 units in 5 hours. How many units will it produce in 8 hours?

Solution:

Units produced per hour = 100 / 5 = 20 units
Units produced in 8 hours = 20 * 8 = 160 units.
Answer: The machine will produce 160 units in 8 hours.

Problem 3:

Tom takes 7 days to paint a house, while Jerry takes 10 days. If they work together, how many days will it take to paint the house?

Solution:

Tom’s one-day work rate = 1/7
Jerry’s one-day work rate = 1/10
Combined work rate = (1/7) + (1/10) = (10 + 7) / 70 = 17 / 70
Time taken = 1 / (17/70) = 70 / 17 ≈ 4.12 days.
Answer: Tom and Jerry together can paint the house in approximately 4.12 days.

Problem 4:

Sarah and Daniel working together can complete a task in 9 days. Sarah alone can complete it in 15 days. How many days will Daniel take to complete it alone?

Solution:

Combined one-day work rate = 1/9
Sarah's one-day work rate = 1/15
Daniel's one-day work rate = 1/9 - 1/15 = (5 - 3) / 45 = 2 / 45
Time taken by Daniel = 1 / (2/45) = 45 / 2 = 22.5 days.
Answer: Daniel alone would take 22.5 days to complete the task.

Problem 5:

A team of 3 people can complete a job in 12 days. If 2 more people join the team, how many days will it take to complete the job?

Solution:

Initial team work rate = 1 job / 12 days = 1/12
Assuming equal efficiency, each person’s rate = (1/12) / 3 = 1/36
New combined rate with 5 people = 5 * (1/36) = 5/36
Time taken = 1 / (5/36) = 36 / 5 = 7.2 days.
Answer: With 2 more people, the job will take approximately 7.2 days to complete.
