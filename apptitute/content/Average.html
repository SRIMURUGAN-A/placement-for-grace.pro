Percentage

Concept Overview:

The concept of percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. It's widely used in real-life scenarios, such as calculating discounts, understanding statistics, and comparing quantities. For example, if you scored 80 out of 100 on a test, your score can be expressed as 80%.

Basic formulas for percentage calculations:

  • Percentage Formula: (Part / Whole) * 100
  • Increase Percentage: ((New Value - Original Value) / Original Value) * 100
  • Decrease Percentage: ((Original Value - New Value) / Original Value) * 100

Types of Problems

Common types of percentage problems include:

  • Finding the percentage of a given quantity.
  • Calculating the percentage increase or decrease.
  • Determining the whole, given a part and its percentage.

Detailed Example

Example Scenario:

Alice got 450 out of 600 in her exams. She wants to calculate her percentage score.

Solution:

- Part = 450
- Whole = 600
- Percentage = (Part / Whole) * 100

Solution Steps:

Percentage = (450 / 600) * 100 = 75%
Answer: Alice scored 75% in her exams.

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

John scored 36 out of 50 on his math test. What is his percentage score?

Solution:

Percentage = (36 / 50) * 100 = 72%
Answer: John's score is 72%.

Problem 2:

A product's price increased from $80 to $100. Find the percentage increase.

Solution:

Increase Percentage = ((100 - 80) / 80) * 100 = 25%
Answer: The percentage increase is 25%.

Problem 3:

A population decreased from 2000 to 1600. Calculate the percentage decrease.

Solution:

Decrease Percentage = ((2000 - 1600) / 2000) * 100 = 20%
Answer: The percentage decrease is 20%.

Problem 4:

Lisa saved $40, which is 20% of her total savings. What is her total savings?

Solution:

Total Savings = Part / (Percentage / 100) = 40 / 0.2 = $200
Answer: Lisa's total savings is $200.

Problem 5:

A shirt's original price was $50, and it was sold for $40. What was the discount percentage?

Solution:

Discount Percentage = ((50 - 40) / 50) * 100 = 20%
Answer: The discount percentage is 20%.
