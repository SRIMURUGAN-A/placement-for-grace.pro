Time and Distance

Concept Overview:

Time and Distance problems involve calculating the time taken, distance covered, or speed based on the given information. These concepts are essential in real-life scenarios, such as determining travel times, vehicle speeds, or planning journeys. The fundamental formula to remember is:

Speed (S) = Distance (D) / Time (T)

Key points to remember:

  • If speed is given in km/h, time should be in hours, and distance in kilometers.
  • If speed is given in m/s, time should be in seconds, and distance in meters.
  • To convert km/h to m/s, multiply by (5/18), and to convert m/s to km/h, multiply by (18/5).

Types of Problems

Some typical types of problems include:

  • Calculating distance, given speed and time.
  • Finding time taken, given speed and distance.
  • Determining speed, given time and distance.

Detailed Example

Example Scenario:

Imagine you are planning a road trip. You know that the distance from your city to the destination is 300 km, and you plan to drive at an average speed of 60 km/h. You want to calculate how long the trip will take.

Solution:

- We need to find the time taken for the journey.
- The distance (D) = 300 km, and the speed (S) = 60 km/h.

Solution Steps:

Time = Distance / Speed
Time = 300 km / 60 km/h = 5 hours
Answer: The journey will take 5 hours.

Practice Problems

Problem 1:

A cyclist travels at a speed of 15 km/h. How long will it take him to cover a distance of 45 km?

Solution:

Speed = 15 km/h, Distance = 45 km.
Time = Distance / Speed = 45 / 15 = 3 hours.
Answer: It will take 3 hours to cover 45 km.

Problem 2:

A car covers a distance of 120 kilometers in 2 hours. What is its speed in km/h?

Solution:

Distance = 120 km, Time = 2 hours.
Speed = Distance / Time = 120 / 2 = 60 km/h.
Answer: The car's speed is 60 km/h.

Problem 3:

John walks at a speed of 4 km/h. How much distance will he cover in 1.5 hours?

Solution:

Speed = 4 km/h, Time = 1.5 hours.
Distance = Speed * Time = 4 * 1.5 = 6 km.
Answer: John will cover 6 km.

Problem 4:

A train travels at 90 km/h and covers a certain distance in 4 hours. What is the distance covered?

Solution:

Speed = 90 km/h, Time = 4 hours.
Distance = Speed * Time = 90 * 4 = 360 km.
Answer: The distance covered is 360 km.

Problem 5:

A person jogs at a speed of 8 m/s. How far will they run in 15 seconds?

Solution:

Speed = 8 m/s, Time = 15 seconds.
Distance = Speed * Time = 8 * 15 = 120 meters.
Answer: The person will run 120 meters.
